Overclocking Tips for CPUs

  • Cooling: Ensure you have a sufficient cooling solution. Liquid cooling is often preferred for higher-end overclocks.
  • Voltage Management: Increasing voltage improves stability but also increases heat. Keep voltages below recommended maximums (usually 1.3-1.35V) to avoid damaging the chip.
  • Stress Testing: After overclocking, always stress test your CPU with tools like Prime95 or AIDA64 to ensure stability under load.
  • BIOS Settings: Use the BIOS to manage multiplier, clock speeds, and voltages. Start with small increments and increase gradually.
